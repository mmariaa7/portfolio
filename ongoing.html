Design of tangible geospatial interactions

This research project, held at Luxembourg Institut of Science and Technology (LIST), aims to develop innovative interactions with GIS (Geographic Information System)-based systems within the context of participatory urban planning. These interactions are designed for tangible tabletops and multi-display environments to enhance collaboration and support effective decision-making processes.

Design of tangible geospatial interactions (in progress)

Our team works with tabletops and transparent tokens to explore innovative ways of interacting with map-based systems. Currently, we are in the ideation phase, during which two functional prototypes featuring one to three tangible interactions will be developed to determine the most effective approach. Thanks to interviews conducted with specialists from diverse fields, we have gathered valuable insights and requirements regarding these systems.

Work done

  • 1 State of the art on Tangible interactions in multi-display environments
  • 4 Interviews with specialists from the following domains : urban planning, logitics, ecology, energy
  • 3 Paper prototypes of a "timeline" feature to test which one is better

Goals

  • 2 High-fidelity functional prototypes using JavaScript and its libraries
  • 2 Sessions of users tests
